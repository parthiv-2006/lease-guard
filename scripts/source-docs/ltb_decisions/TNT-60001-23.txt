ONTARIO LANDLORD AND TENANT BOARD
File Number: TNT-60001-23

BETWEEN:
Applicant Tenant
and
Respondent Landlord

REASONS FOR ORDER

BACKGROUND
The Tenant applied for repayment of a key deposit after the Landlord retained it in full at the end of the tenancy, claiming the Tenant had failed to return a key fob and a parking access card.

ANALYSIS
The tenancy agreement required a key deposit of $200 for one key fob and one parking access card. The Tenant returned the key fob but not the parking access card. The Landlord retained the entire $200 deposit.

Section 105(1) of the Act permits a landlord to require a key deposit but limits the deposit to an amount not exceeding the expected cost of replacing the keys. Section 105(3) requires the landlord to return the key deposit at the end of the tenancy if the keys are returned.

Two issues arise. First, the Board must assess whether $200 represents the expected replacement cost of the items. Evidence before the Board established that a replacement parking access card costs approximately $25. The key deposit for the access card portion is excessive if the $200 deposit was not tied to actual replacement costs.

Second, the Landlord is entitled to retain only the cost of replacing the unreturned item — the parking access card — not the entire deposit. Retaining the full deposit when only one of two items was not returned is inconsistent with section 105, which limits retention to the cost of replacing missing keys.

PRINCIPLE
A key deposit may only be retained in an amount equal to the actual replacement cost of keys not returned at the end of the tenancy, under section 105 of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006. A landlord cannot retain the entire key deposit if only some keys are unreturned; retention must be proportionate to replacement cost.

ORDER
The Landlord is ordered to repay the key deposit minus $25 (the actual cost of replacing the parking access card).
