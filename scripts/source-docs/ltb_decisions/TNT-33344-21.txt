BACKGROUND

The Tenant applied alleging the Landlord collected an illegal deposit. The lease required payment of one month's rent as a last month's rent deposit, which both parties agree is permitted. The Landlord also required a pet deposit of $600 because the Tenant has a cat. The Landlord characterized this as compensation for the additional wear associated with having a pet.

ANALYSIS

Section 105(1) of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 limits deposits to one month's rent as a rent deposit. There is no provision permitting a separate pet deposit. Section 134(1) prohibits collecting any charge not authorized by the Act.

While the landlord's concern about pet damage is understandable, the Act does not create an exception for pet deposits. The Landlord's remedy for any damage caused by the Tenant's pet is a claim under section 89 after the tenancy ends. The $600 pet deposit is an illegal charge.

The Tenant correctly notes that section 14 of the RTA voids any provision in a tenancy agreement that prohibits pets or charges additional fees related to pets. A pet deposit falls squarely within the prohibited charges under section 14 read together with section 134.

ORDER

1. The application is granted.
2. The Landlord shall pay the Tenant $600.00, being the pet deposit collected in violation of sections 105, 134, and 14 of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006.
3. The Landlord shall pay the Tenant $48.00 for the cost of filing the application.
