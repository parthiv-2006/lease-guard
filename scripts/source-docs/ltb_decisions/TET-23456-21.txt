BACKGROUND

The Tenant applied for an abatement following a leaking kitchen sink pipe that caused water damage to the cabinet below. The Tenant reported the leak on March 1, 2021. The Landlord repaired the pipe on March 22, 2021 — 21 days after notification. During this time the cabinet was unusable and water had to be caught in buckets.

ANALYSIS

Section 20(1) of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 requires the Landlord to maintain the rental unit in a good state of repair. A leaking pipe causing active water damage is a maintenance issue the Landlord must address promptly upon notice.

The 21-day delay was unreasonable for a plumbing issue causing ongoing water damage. A prompt response — within 3-5 business days for non-emergency plumbing issues — is expected under section 20. The Landlord offered no compelling explanation for the delay.

However, the issue, while a genuine maintenance failure, was not so severe as to substantially affect the habitability of the unit. The Tenant continued to occupy the unit and use the kitchen. I find an abatement of 10% of rent for the 21-day period is appropriate. At $1,400/month ($46.67/day): $46.67 × 21 days × 10% = $98.00. I also award $48.00 for filing costs.

ORDER

1. The application is granted in part.
2. The Landlord shall pay to the Tenant $98.00 as an abatement of rent for the 21-day period of unremediated water damage.
3. The Landlord shall pay to the Tenant $48.00 for the cost of filing the application.
4. The total amount the Landlord shall pay is $146.00.
