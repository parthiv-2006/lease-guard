BACKGROUND

The Tenant applied under section 135 of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 for a rent rebate. The Tenant alleges the Landlord increased the rent without giving proper notice under section 116 and without complying with the rent increase guideline.

The monthly rent was $1,700. In January 2022 the Landlord informed the Tenant by email that rent would increase to $1,950 effective March 1, 2022. The Tenant paid the increased rent for March and April 2022 but then filed this application.

The 2022 rent increase guideline was 1.2%.

ANALYSIS

Section 116(1) of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 provides that a landlord shall not increase the rent charged to a tenant for a rental unit without first giving the tenant at least 90 days' written notice of the rent increase. The notice must be in the form approved by the Board.

The Landlord's email notification did not meet these requirements on two independent grounds. First, the email was sent on January 2, 2022 for a March 1 increase — only 58 days' notice, well below the mandatory 90-day minimum. Second, an email is not a written notice in the approved form; the Landlord is required to use the Form N1.

Section 120(1) further provides that no landlord shall increase the rent charged to a tenant by more than the guideline without authorization under section 126. The 2022 guideline was 1.2%. A lawful increase on a $1,700 monthly rent would be $20.40, bringing rent to $1,720.40. The actual increase was $250 per month — approximately 14.7% — far exceeding the guideline.

The rent increase is void for two independent reasons: insufficient notice and exceeding the guideline without above-guideline authorization. The Tenant is entitled to a rebate of the excess rent paid: $230 per month (the amount above $1,720 guideline rent) × 2 months = $460.

ORDER

1. The application is granted.
2. The purported rent increase to $1,950 effective March 1, 2022 is void for failure to comply with sections 116 and 120 of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006.
3. The lawful rent remains $1,700 per month.
4. The Landlord shall pay the Tenant $460.00, being the excess rent paid in March and April 2022.
5. The Landlord shall pay the Tenant $48.00 for the cost of filing the application.
