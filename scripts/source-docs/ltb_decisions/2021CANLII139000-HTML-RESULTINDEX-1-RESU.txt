# Determinations:
****
**
1. The Landlord is seeking
eviction of the Tenant because the Landlord has received anumber of complaints about the smell ofmarijuanaemanating from the Tenant’s rentalunit.
**
2. The Landlord served the
Tenant with two N5 Notices of Termination.The
Landlord’sagent, B. Bidgood (BB),
said that there were no further complaints about the Tenant’smarijuanasmoking in the week of January
13, 2021, after the Landlord served the first N5Notice ofTermination.The complaintsstarted againin
February, 2021.
**
3. Consequently,IfindthattheTenantvoidedthefirstN5NoticeofTermination.
**
4. BB said that the
Landlord received complaints from tenants of different rental units aboutthemarijuanasmoking of the Tenant.She said that she contacted the
complainingtenants to testify at the
hearing, but they were afraid of reprisals by the Tenant so theyrefusedtoattendthehearing.
5. BB said that the
complaints were all about the smell ofmarijuanain the stairwells to theexterior of the building, and an intense
smell coming from the rental unit, bothering otherresidentsoftheresidential
complex.
6. The Landlord submitted
into evidence letters sent to the Tenant, explaining to the Tenanthowhecould consumemarijuanawithoutbotheringotherresidentsofthebuilding.
7. BB said that the Tenant
told her he had a medical requirement to consumemarijuana, buthe neversubmittedanydocumentationto supporthiscontention.
8. BB also said that the
complaints have continued, and that the Landlord received a newcomplaint shortly before the hearing date.She said that the complaints primarily
focus onthe noxious smell of themarijuanadisturbing residents’ reasonable enjoyment of theirrentalunits ortheresidential complex.
9. The Landlord submits
that the Tenant should be evicted because he has been interferingwith other tenants’ reasonable enjoyment
of the residential complex.The
Landlord alsosaid that itwould considerthe issuanceofa conditional order.
ReasonsandAnalysis:
10.I am mindful of the fact that none of the
complaining residents attended the hearing toprovide evidence about the effect of the Tenant’smarijuanasmoking on
their reasonableenjoyment.
11.The Landlord’s witness testified that she
had received complaints on numerous datesfrom
different residents about the noxious smell ofmarijuanabeing emitted from theTenant’srental unit.
12.BB provided documentary evidence of the
Landlord’s various communications with theTenant
about the complaints, and the Landlord’s request for documentary evidence fromthe Tenant about his medical requirement
to smokemarijuanain the rental unit.TheLandlord sent letters to the Tenant on
November 10, 2020, December 29, 2020,December
30, 2020, and January 13, 2021, informing the Tenant about the multiple andongoing complaints from residents, and
requesting documentary evidence from theTenant
abouthismedicalconditionandrequirements.
13.The Landlord only received replies from the
Tenant on December 29 and December 30,2020,
in which the Tenant asserted that he had to smokemarijuanafor his cancer, andthat the Landlord was harassing him with
its request to cease smokingmarijuana.TheTenant also threatened to contact the
police about the harassment.He told
the Landlordto directall furthercommunicationthroughhislawyer.
14.The Landlord’s letter of January 13, 2021,
states that the Landlord attempted to contactthe lawyer’s number provided by the Tenant, but it was a personal phone
number, andtheLandlordwasalsounable tofindalawyerwith thenameprovided bytheTenant.
15.IfindthatBB’sevidenceaboutthecomplaintsofothertenantswashearsayevidence.
16.Subsection 64(1) of the*Residential
Tenancies Act, 2006*(the ‘Act’) provides that alandlord may give a tenant notice of termination of the tenancy
if the tenant, a guest, oranother
occupant of the rental unit’s conduct is such that it substantially interferes
withthe reasonableenjoymentoftheresidentialcomplex.
17.The evidence provided
by BB was hearsay evidence, based on complaints that she hadreceived from other tenants about the
Tenant’s conduct. BB provided dates and detailsof the complaintsshe
received, andherevidence wasuncontested.
18.It is accepted law that pursuant to section
183 of the Act, and subsection 15(1) of the*Statutory
Powers Procedure Act, R.S.O. 1990,*a tribunal may admit any oral testimony
ifit is relevant to the subject
matter of the proceeding, and if it permits the tribunal to adoptthemostexpeditiousmethodofdeterminingthequestionsarising ina proceeding.
19.BB’s testimony was relevant.She had received numerous complaints about
the Tenant’smarijuanasmoking, and
she had sent written communication to the Tenant asking abouthis conduct, and also requesting medical
documentation to prove why he needed tosmokemarijuanain the rental unit.The
Tenant did not provide this evidence, and merelyreplied withthreats
againsttheLandlord.
20.BB’s hearsay testimony was uncontested.She also said that she had attempted to
havethe complainants testify, but
they were afraid of reprisals.The
Landlord had attempted tocommunicate
with the Tenant on several occasions.With
its letters, the Landlord wascarrying
out the obligations of the Landlord to provide residents with reasonableenjoyment of their rental units and the
residential complex, and the Landlord attempted toopen a dialogue with the Tenant for him to justify his
substantial interference with others.The
Tenant refused to discuss the matter with the Landlord, and he failed to
providedocumentary evidence to
substantiate why he had to interfere with others’ reasonableenjoyment oftheresidential
complex.
21.For the above-mentioned reasons, I accept
BB’s hearsay evidence about the substantialinterference caused by the Tenant’s conduct over a number of months,
since at leastNovember, 2020,andcontinuingto thedateofthe hearing.
22.Consequently, I find that the Tenant, an
occupant of the Tenant, or a guest of the Tenant,has substantially interfered with the reasonable enjoyment of
other tenants of theresidentialcomplexwithhismarijuanasmoking.
23.The Landlord is seeking eviction, however,
they said that they would consider aconditional
order prohibiting the Tenant from smoking in his rental unit and therebydisturbingothertenants.
24.I find it is appropriate to issue a
conditional order in order to give the Tenant anopportunitytopreservehis tenancy.

circumstances above in accordance withsubsection
83(2) of the*Residential Tenancies Act, 2006*(the 'Act'), and find that
it wouldnot be unfair to grant
