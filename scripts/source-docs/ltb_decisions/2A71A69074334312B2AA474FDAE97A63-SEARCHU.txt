# Determinations:
****
**
1.The Landlord’s L3 application
filed on October 27, 2021 was based on aN9notice of termination signed by the
Tenant on September 1, 2021 with a termination date of November30,2021pursuanttosubsection77(1)ofthe*ResidentialTenanciesAct,2006*.
PRELIMINARYISSUE
**
2.At the hearing, the Tenant’s
representative raised a preliminary issue with respect to the validity of theN9notice of termination – specifically that it did not comply with the
Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 as it failed to identify the rental unit and
sought, on this basis,thatthemotiontosetasidetheorderbegrantedandtheLandlord’sapplicationbedismissed.
**
3.The Tenant relies on three cases: EAL-19177-SA,
NOL-22245-16 and*George V ApartmentLtd.v.Cobb*,[2002]O.J.No.5918andsubmitsthatwherethenoticedoesnot comply with the Act, it is rendered
invalid – notices of termination cannot be amended.
**
4.The Landlord submits that the
residential complex is a house in which the Tenant resides inthebasementunit.TheLandlordresidesonthemainlevel.TheLandlordfurthersubmits thatthenoticeofterminationidentifiedtherentalunitasitislistedas“73HORSELEYHILL DRIVE”
and that it wasunderstood bythe parties that the Tenant was making
reference to thebasementunit in which she resides.
**
5.The Landlord’s representative submits that the case
law submitted by the Tenant’s representative differs from the particulars
before the Board as the Board cases EAL- 19177-SA and NOL-222465-16 both dealt
with the issue of coercion and intimidation whereas*GeorgeVApartmentLtd.v.Cobb*,[2002]O.J.No.5918dealtwithaverbalnotice of termination.
**
6.TheLandlordmaintainsthepositionthattheN9providedwasvalidandthatthe preliminary motion should be
dismissed.
ANALYSIS
**
7.Section43(1)oftheActstates:
43(1)WherethisActpermitsalandlordortenanttogiveanoticeoftermination,the notice**shall**be in a form
approved by the Board and shall,
# (a) identifytherentalunitforwhichthenoticeisgiven;
****
(b) statethedateonwhichthetenancyistoterminate;and
(c) besignedbythepersongivingthenotice,ortheperson’sagent. [Emphasis added.]
**
8.Based on the evidence before the
Board, it is undisputed that the Tenant resides in thebasementunit. It isalso undisputedthat the rentalunit
“basement”isnot identifiedon theN9notice of termination that was given by the Tenant to the Landlords on
September 1, 2021.WhiletheLandlord
submitsthatthiswasunderstoodbetweentheparties–Idonot find that this
“understanding” meets the mandatory requirements under the Act nor do I find it
equates to substantial compliance under section 212 of the Act.
**
9.Inthecaseof*ReBianchiandAguanno*,[1983 CanLII 1967 (ON SC)](https://www.canlii.org/en/on/onsc/doc/1983/1983canlii1967/1983canlii1967.html),[1983]O.J.No.3053,42O.R.(2d)76,whichwas based on a landlords notice of
termination,the Ontario Divisional
Court dealt with the equivalent of section 43 of the Act, which was section 44
under the previous*Landlord and Tenant Act*and found thatcompliance
is mandatory and cannot be amended by the court.A notice of termination
contravening this section is void.
**
10.In the Divisional Court case of*George V Apartment
Ltd. v. Cobb*,[2002] O.J. No. 5918whichwascitedbytheTenant’srepresentative,andwhichwasbasedonaverbalnoticeof termination, as correctly pointed out by the Landlord’s
representative, the court states at paragraph 15:
[15] More broadly, we find the
scheme of the Act should be applied with an even handbetweenlandlordandtenant
whenitcomestonoticesoftermination.Section
39(1) of the Act recites that a
tenancy may be terminated only in accordance with the Act. A tenant may
terminate a tenancy at the end of a period of the tenancy by giving notice of
termination to the landlord. (s. 46 of the Act) … Section 43(1) sets outtheformofthenoticethatistobegivenbyeitheralandlordoratenant.Wefind the notice requirement
should apply equally to landlord and to tenant. There is nothing in the
legislation to suggest that a tenant's notice which fails to comply with the
provisions of the Act is other than void.
**
11.Fromthecaselaw,Iconcludethatwhereanoticeofterminationdoesnotcomplywiththe mandatory
requirements of the Act, it is deemed invalid. In the case before the Board, I find
that theN9notice of termination fails to identify the rental unit,
contravening subsection 43(1)(a) of the Act and is rendered defective; as such,
the Tenant’s motion to setasidetheordermustbegrantedandtheLandlord’sL3applicationmustbedismissed.
**
12.Thisordercontainsallofthereasonsforthedecisionwithinit.Nofurtherreasonsshallbeissued.
# Itis
orderedthat:
****
1. ThemotiontosetasideorderTEL-20348-21issuedonNovember29,2021isgranted.
2. TheLandlord’sL3applicationisdismissed.
