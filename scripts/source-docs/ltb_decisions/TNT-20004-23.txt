ONTARIO LANDLORD AND TENANT BOARD
File Number: TNT-20004-23

BETWEEN:
Applicant Tenant
and
Respondent Landlord

REASONS FOR ORDER

BACKGROUND
The Tenant applied for a determination that a clause requiring monthly "utility administration fees" of $30 separate from rent and separate from actual utility costs was unlawful.

ANALYSIS
The tenancy agreement stated: "In addition to monthly rent, the Tenant shall pay a utility administration fee of $30.00 per month to cover the Landlord's costs of administering utility accounts, billing, and coordination."

The Board finds this charge void under section 134(1) of the Act. The utility administration fee is not rent. It is not a permitted deposit. It is an additional recurring charge imposed on the tenant for the landlord's administrative overhead in managing utility accounts.

The Act defines "rent" in section 2(1) to include the amount paid for the right to occupy the rental unit and any services or facilities provided by the landlord. If the landlord provides utilities or utility administration as a service, the cost of doing so should be incorporated into the rent, not charged separately as an administrative fee.

The Landlord cannot structure a prohibited additional charge as a "fee" or "administration cost" and thereby circumvent the prohibition in section 134. The form of the charge does not change its substance — it is an amount collected in addition to rent, which the Act prohibits.

PRINCIPLE
A utility administration fee, billing coordination fee, or any recurring administrative charge imposed in addition to rent is a prohibited additional charge under section 134(1) of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006. Administrative costs associated with providing services must be incorporated into the rent, not charged separately.

ORDER
The Landlord is ordered to cease collecting the utility administration fee and to repay all such fees collected during the tenancy.
