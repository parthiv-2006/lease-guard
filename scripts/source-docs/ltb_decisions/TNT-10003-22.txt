ONTARIO LANDLORD AND TENANT BOARD
File Number: TNT-10003-22

BETWEEN:
Applicant Tenant
and
Respondent Landlord

REASONS FOR ORDER

BACKGROUND
The Tenant sought repayment of three months' rent retained by the Landlord after the Tenant provided notice of early termination under a fixed-term lease. The Landlord relied on a "penalty deposit" clause to retain the funds.

ANALYSIS
The tenancy agreement included: "The Tenant shall provide a penalty deposit of three months' rent, to be forfeited by the Tenant in the event of early termination of this agreement."

The Board makes the following findings:

1. The clause creates a deposit of three months' rent. Section 105 of the Act permits only a last month's rent deposit of no more than one month's rent. Three months' rent deposit is void on its face.

2. Even if the deposit were of lawful amount, a forfeiture clause constitutes both an excess deposit (section 105) and a prohibited charge (section 134). A landlord cannot structure a penalty as a deposit to circumvent the prohibition on early termination fees.

3. Section 3 of the Act provides that any tenancy agreement term inconsistent with the Act is void. The penalty deposit clause is inconsistent with multiple provisions of the Act simultaneously.

The Board rejects the Landlord's characterization of the forfeiture as "agreed damages." Ontario residential tenancy law does not recognize agreed damages clauses that override the statutory framework for determining remedies.

PRINCIPLE
A "penalty deposit" or any amount held as security for a tenant's continued occupancy for a fixed term is void under the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006. A landlord may not use deposit mechanics to achieve what is in substance an early termination penalty.

ORDER
The Landlord is ordered to return all funds retained as a penalty deposit, less the lawful last month's rent deposit which may be properly applied to the final period.
