ONTARIO LANDLORD AND TENANT BOARD
File Number: TNT-30003-23

BETWEEN:
Applicant Tenant
and
Respondent Landlord

REASONS FOR ORDER

BACKGROUND
The Tenant applied for a determination that a clause purporting to require the Tenant to pay the Landlord's legal costs in any dispute — regardless of outcome — was void and unenforceable.

ANALYSIS
The tenancy agreement stated: "In the event of any dispute between the parties, the Tenant agrees to reimburse the Landlord for all reasonable legal costs and fees incurred by the Landlord in connection with such dispute, regardless of the outcome of any proceeding."

The Board finds this clause void on two independent grounds.

First, section 134(1) of the Act prohibits a landlord from charging or collecting any amount other than rent. A mandatory cost-indemnification clause is a prohibited additional charge — it imposes a financial obligation on the tenant beyond rent, and it does so regardless of whether the landlord prevails.

Second, the clause is inconsistent with the Board's authority over costs under section 204 of the Act. The Board has discretion to award costs in exceptional circumstances. A pre-agreed contractual costs clause that operates independently of the Board's discretion is inconsistent with the statutory scheme and void under section 3.

The practical effect of the clause — requiring a tenant to pay the landlord's legal costs before any dispute is even resolved — would deter tenants from exercising their statutory rights. A clause that chills the exercise of statutory rights is contrary to the purpose of the Act as a consumer protection statute.

PRINCIPLE
A clause requiring a tenant to pay a landlord's legal costs, dispute expenses, or attorney fees in connection with any tenancy dispute is void under sections 3, 134, and 204 of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006. Costs in Board proceedings are governed exclusively by the Board's statutory discretion.

ORDER
The Board declares the legal cost indemnification clause void and of no effect.
