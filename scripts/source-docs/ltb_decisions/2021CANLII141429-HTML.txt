# Determinations:
****
**
1. The Tenant challenged
the method of service of the N12 Notice of Termination. Theprescribed method of service is not
exclusive to those identified in the Act and it may beserved via other methods as along as a party can establishes
that the it came other’sattention.
This is permitted by the Board’s Rules of Procedure.On a balance ofprobabilities,IamsatisfiedtheN12 Noticewasissuedandthe
Tenantreceivedit.
**
2. The Landlords’ son
provided an affidavit and testimony that he requires the rental unit forthepurpose
of residential occupation.
**
3. TheLandlord hasattemptedtopaytheTenantcompensationequaltoonemonth'srenton
November 27, 2020 by e-transfer which the Tenant elected not to accept. TheLandlord’s attempt adequately demonstrates
their intention to pay the one-monthcompensationbeforethedateofterminationontheN12NoticetocomplywiththeAct.
**FileNumber:**CEL-96903-20
4. Ihaveconsideredallofthedisclosedcircumstancesinaccordancewith subsection83(2)of the*Residential
Tenancies Act, 2006*(the 'Act'), and find that it would not be unfair tograntrelief
from eviction pursuanttosubsection 83(1)ofthe Act.
5. The courts have said
that the termination of a tenancy is a remedy of last resort and reliefshouldbegrantedwherethetenancycanbesavedwithoutoverlyprejudicingalandlord’sinterests.
6. In this case, the
tenancy between the Tenant and Landlord has been longstanding since2009. The Tenant works at the airport and
his hours reduced because of the Covid-19pandemic.
The pandemic has impacted his income and he can’t afford market rent. TheTenant’s right to security of tenure
outweighs the Landlord’s interest given his son liveswith theLandlordsandtheyownotherproperties.
# Itis orderedthat:
****
1. Theapplicationisdismissed.
