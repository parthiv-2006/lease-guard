ONTARIO LANDLORD AND TENANT BOARD
File Number: TNT-20001-23

BETWEEN:
Applicant Tenant
and
Respondent Landlord

REASONS FOR ORDER

BACKGROUND
The Tenant applied for a determination that a "late payment administrative fee" of $75 collected each time rent was received after the first of the month was an unlawful charge under the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006.

ANALYSIS
The tenancy agreement contained the clause: "In the event rent is not received by the first day of each month, the Tenant shall pay an administrative fee of $75 for each late payment, in addition to the monthly rent."

Section 134(1) of the Act provides that no landlord shall, directly or indirectly, charge or collect any amount of money, including a fee, fine or penalty, other than rent. A late payment fee is precisely the kind of additional charge this section prohibits. The fee is not rent; it is a financial penalty for a failure to pay rent on time.

The Board further notes that the Act contains its own regime for late payment of rent. A landlord whose tenant pays rent late may serve a Notice to Terminate for non-payment of rent under section 59. The Act does not supplement this regime with a contractual right to collect administrative fees.

The Landlord's argument that the fee was reasonable compensation for administrative inconvenience is rejected. The reasonableness of the amount is irrelevant when the charge is categorically prohibited by section 134.

PRINCIPLE
A late payment fee, administrative fee for late rent, or any financial penalty imposed when rent is not received by a specific date is a prohibited additional charge under section 134(1) of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006. The landlord's remedy for late-paying tenants is the statutory notice and hearing process, not a contractual fee.

ORDER
The Landlord is ordered to repay all late payment administrative fees collected from the Tenant, plus interest from the date of collection.
