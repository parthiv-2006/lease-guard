# Determinations:
****
**
1. SL is a Senior
Superintendent at the residential complex. He attended the rental unit onNovember 4, 2020 and on January 8, 2020.
On those dates, there was a largeaccumulation
of garbage and dirt throughout the rental unit and the unit was veryunhygienic. SL took photographs which were
submitted into evidence and supported histestimony.
**
2. RC is a Community
Services Coordinator. He attended the rental unit on February 8,2021 and on February 18, 2021. On those
dates, there was a large accumulation ofgarbage
and dirt throughout the rental unit and the unit was very unhygienic. In
addition,the stove was left on and
there were flammable items on the stove each day. SL tookphotographswhichwere submittedintoevidence
andsupportedhistestimony.
3. CB works with the
Tenant Resolution Office. She attended the rental unit on March 18,2021 with the Toronto Fire Department. On
that day, there continued to be a largeaccumulation
of garbage and dirt throughout the rental unit and it continued to be veryunhygienic.
4. The Landlord has filed
two previous applications with respect to thecleanlinessof therental unit and has obtained orders
requiring the Tenant to rectify the situation (orderTNL-88687-16 issued on February 2, 2017 and TNL-05047-18 issued
on August 21,2018).
5. Based on the evidence
before me, I accept that the rental unit is in an unsanitary statebecause of excessive dirt and clutter and
that it is not in a state ofordinarycleanlinessasrequiredby section33of
the Act.
6. I find that the Tenant
substantially interfered with the reasonable enjoyment of theresidential complex by the Landlord and
with the lawful rights and privileges of theLandlord by failing to maintain the rental unit in a state ofordinarycleanliness.TheLandlord served a first N5 notice of
termination on January 26, 2021, and the Tenant didnotvoid thenoticewithin theseven-dayperiod setoutin thenotice.
7. Based on the evidence
before me, I also accept that the conditions in the rental unitconstitutea fire andhealth
hazard.
8. I find that the Tenant
has seriously impaired the safety of another person because theconditionoftherentalunitcreatesfirehazardsandhealthandsafetyhazardstotheotherresidentsin the complex as wellastotheTenanthimself.
9. The Landlord requested
an order requiring the Tenant to return the rental unit to a stateofordinarycleanlinessas soon as
possible and no later than May 31, 2021 failing whichthe Landlord could, without notice to the Tenant, apply to the
Board pursuant to section78of theact foranorderterminating
thetenancy andevicting theTenant.
10.Ihaveconsideredallofthedisclosedcircumstancesinaccordancewith subsection83(2)of the*Residential
Tenancies Act, 2006*(the 'Act'), and find that it would not be unfair togrant relief from eviction subject tothe condition(s) set out in this order
pursuant tosubsection83(1)(a) and 204(1) ofthe Act.
