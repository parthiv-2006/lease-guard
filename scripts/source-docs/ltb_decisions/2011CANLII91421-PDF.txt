Order under Section 31
Residential Tenancies Act, 2006
File Number: TNT-23273-11
B. B. (the 'Tenant') applied for an order determining that J. R. (the 'Landlord') substantially
interfered with the reasonable enjoyment of the rental unit or residential complex by the Tenant or
by a member of his household and withheld or deliberately interfered with the reasonable supply
of a vital service that the Landlord is obligated to supply under the tenancy agreement.
This application was heard in Newmarket on November 24, 2011.
The Tenant and the Landlord attended the hearing.
Determinations:
1. The rental unit is a basement apartment in a house. The upper floor is occupied by other
tenants. The Landlord does not reside in the house.
2. The Tenant moved into the rental unit on May 1, 2010. He pays $700.00 per month for
rent.
3. The programmable thermostat for the central heating for the entire house, including the
Tenant’s basement apartment, is located in the upstairs rental unit.
4. During the winter of 2011, specifically from February 2011 to May 2011, the tenants in the
upstairs unit adjusted the thermostat so that there was inadequate heat to the Tenant’s
rental unit.
5. The Tenant advised the Landlord of this issue. On February 13, 2011, the Landlord wrote
to the Tenant, with a copy to the upstairs tenant, setting out the temperature at which the
thermostat was to be set.
6. The upstairs tenants did not comply with the Landlord’s letter.
7. Despite the Tenant’s further complaints, the Landlord did not enclose the programmable
thermostat in a locked “bubble”, preset to the appropriate temperature settings, nor did he
take any further steps to ensure the Tenant had adequate heat. The Landlord’s
explanation was that the upstairs tenants were very difficult and unco-operative, and if he
had installed a locking device on the thermostat they would just have broken it. The
Landlord further indicated that the basement had electric baseboard heaters that the
Tenant could have used if he wished. The Tenant is responsible to pay for electrical use,
and would have incurred substantial additional expense if he had used the electric
baseboard heaters.
8. I find that the Landlord failed to take appropriate steps to ensure that there was adequate
heat from the central heating system to the rental unit. If the main floor tenants interfered

with the supply of heat to the basement, the Landlord should have taken steps to prevent
such interference, like locking the thermostat, or serving the upstairs tenants with a
Notice of Termination based on their interference with the Tenant’s reasonable enjoyment
of his rental unit. It is no answer to say that the Tenant should incur substantial additional
expense to heat his rental unit with electric baseboard heaters, when the central heating
system should have provided adequate heat.
9. I find that the Tenant is entitled to an abatement of rent of 15% for each of the months of
February 2011 to May 2011 inclusive, due to inadequate heat.
10. During the summer months, the tenants in the house were to share the use of the deck,
with each of them having use of half of the deck. This was confirmed by a letter from the
Landlord to the tenants in May 2011.
11. The upstairs tenants did not adhere to the Landlord’s instructions, and used the whole of
the rear deck rather than half, thereby depriving the Tenant of the use of the deck during
the summer months of 2011. Once again the Landlord, while acknowledging the difficult,
unco-operative and selfish nature of the upstairs tenants, failed to take any action to
enable the Tenant to have the use of the deck to which he was entitled.
12. I find that the Tenant is entitled to an abatement of rent of $50.00 per month for the
months of July and August for being deprived of the use of the deck.
It is ordered that:
1. The Landlord shall pay the Tenant an abatement of rent of $420.00 relating to the lack of
heat (60% of $700.00). The Landlord shall also pay the Tenant an abatement of rent of
$100.00 relating to the Tenant’s inability to use the deck.
2. The total amount the Landlord owes is $520.00.
3. The Landlord shall pay the Tenant the full amount owing by December 12, 2011.
4. If the Landlord does not pay the Tenant the full amount owing by December 12, 2011, he
will owe interest. This will be simple interest calculated from December 13, 2011 at 3.00%
annually on the balance outstanding.
5. The Tenant has the right, at any time, to collect the full amount owing or any balance
outstanding under this order.
