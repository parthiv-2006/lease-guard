ONTARIO LANDLORD AND TENANT BOARD
File Number: TNT-30001-23

BETWEEN:
Applicant Tenant
and
Respondent Landlord

REASONS FOR ORDER

BACKGROUND
The Tenant applied for a determination that a mandatory arbitration clause in the tenancy agreement, purporting to require all disputes to be resolved by private arbitration rather than the Landlord and Tenant Board, was void and unenforceable.

ANALYSIS
The tenancy agreement stated: "Any dispute arising under or in connection with this tenancy agreement shall be resolved exclusively by binding private arbitration administered by a mutually agreed arbitrator. The parties waive any right to bring a claim before any tribunal, board, or court."

The Board has jurisdiction to hear this application under section 29 of the Act. The question is whether the mandatory arbitration clause validly displaces this jurisdiction.

Section 168(2) of the Act provides that the Board has exclusive jurisdiction to determine all applications brought under the Act. This jurisdiction cannot be contracted away. The scheme of the Act — conferring comprehensive jurisdiction on the Board over residential tenancy disputes — is a matter of legislative policy that private parties cannot override by agreement.

Section 3 of the Act provides that any provision of a tenancy agreement that is inconsistent with the Act is void. A clause that purports to eliminate the Board's jurisdiction is directly inconsistent with the statutory scheme and is void.

The Board further notes that tenants are often in a weaker bargaining position and that mandatory arbitration clauses in residential tenancy agreements can deprive tenants of the consumer protections embedded in the Board's process, including no-cost applications and the Board's expertise in residential tenancy law.

PRINCIPLE
A mandatory arbitration clause, alternative dispute resolution requirement, or any clause purporting to divert residential tenancy disputes away from the Landlord and Tenant Board is void under sections 3 and 168 of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006. The Board's jurisdiction over residential tenancy disputes is exclusive and cannot be waived by contract.

ORDER
The Board declares the mandatory arbitration clause void and of no effect. This application proceeds before the Board on its merits.
