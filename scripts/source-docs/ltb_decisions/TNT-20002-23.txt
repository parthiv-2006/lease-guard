ONTARIO LANDLORD AND TENANT BOARD
File Number: TNT-20002-23

BETWEEN:
Applicant Tenant
and
Respondent Landlord

REASONS FOR ORDER

BACKGROUND
The Tenant applied under section 135 of the Act for repayment of a "NSF fee" of $50 charged each time a cheque was returned for non-sufficient funds, arguing this constituted an additional charge beyond what the Act permits.

ANALYSIS
The tenancy agreement stated: "The Tenant shall pay a $50.00 NSF administration fee for each returned cheque or failed electronic payment, payable immediately upon notification."

Section 134(1) of the Act prohibits a landlord from charging or collecting any amount other than rent. The question before the Board is whether an NSF fee falls within a permitted exception or is a prohibited charge.

The Board finds that an NSF fee is a prohibited additional charge. It is not rent. It is not a rent deposit or a key deposit as defined by the Act. The Act contains no provision authorizing landlords to charge NSF fees, regardless of actual bank charges incurred.

The Board acknowledges that a returned cheque may cause administrative inconvenience. However, the Act's prohibition on additional charges is broad and categorical. The landlord's remedy when a tenant's payment fails is to pursue the unpaid rent through the statutory process, not to add contractual surcharges.

PRINCIPLE
An NSF (non-sufficient funds) fee, returned payment fee, or failed payment administrative charge is a prohibited additional charge under section 134(1) of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006. No amount may be charged to a tenant beyond rent, a last month's rent deposit, and a key deposit within prescribed limits.

ORDER
The Landlord is ordered to repay all NSF fees collected from the Tenant.
