# Determinations:
****
**
1. The
Landlord brings this A2 application pursuant to subsection 100(1) of the*ResidentialTenanciesAct, 2006*(the
'Act') whichstates:
If a tenant
transfers the occupancy of a rental unit to a person in a manner otherthan by an assignment authorized under
section 95 or a subletting authorized undersection 97, the landlord may apply to the Board for an order terminating
the tenancyand evicting the tenant
and the person to whom occupancy of the rental unit wastransferred.
2. Subsection 100(2)
requires that an application of this nature must be filed within sixtydaysaftertheLandlorddiscoverstheunauthorizedoccupancy.Thisapplicationwasfiledon June24,2020.
3. Thelegaltestonthistypeofanapplicationisasfollows:
(a) DidtheTenanttransferoccupancyoftherentalunittoanotherpersonwithouttheLandlord’sconsentascontemplatedin subsection100(1)oftheAct?
(b) DidtheLandlordfilethisapplicationwithinsixtydaysofdiscoveringtheunauthorizedoccupancyasrequired bysubsection100(2)oftheAct?
(c) If the Tenant did
transfer occupancy and the Landlord filed the application in time,shouldtheTenantbegrantedrelieffromeviction pursuanttosection83oftheAct
4. The Landlord’s
uncontested evidence was the Tenant was the only Tenant on thetenancy agreement. This was confirmed by a
copy of the tenancy agreement. It was alsouncontested
that the Landlord learned that the Tenant passed away on or about June 6,and that the Landlord also learned on the
same date that the Unauthorized Occupantswere
residingintherental unit.
5. Assignments under
section 95 of the Act and sublets under section 97 of the Act requirethe Landlord’s consent. In this case, it
was uncontested that the Landlord did not consentto the transfer of occupancy and in fact asked the Unauthorized
Occupants to vacate therental unit
once they were discovered. Although the Unauthorized Occupants refusedbecause of the COVID-19 pandemic, which
prompted the Landlord to file the application,this does not change the fact that there was a transfer of occupancy in
a manner otherthan by an assignment
under section 95 or a subletting under section 97. TheUnauthorized Occupants were discovered on June 6, 2020 and the
Landlord filed theapplication on
June 24, 2020. This is less than 60-days from the date of discovery. TheLandlord has satisfiedthelegaltestundersection100oftheAct.
6. The Landlord also
claimed compensation under subsection 100(3) of the Act, whichprovides:
A landlord who
makes an application under subsection (1) may also apply to theBoardforanorderforthepaymentofcompensationbytheunauthorizedoccupantfor the use
and occupation of the rental unit, if the unauthorized occupant is inpossessionoftherental unitatthetimetheapplicationis made.
7. It was uncontested that
the Unauthorized Occupants were in possession of the rental unitwhen the Landlord filed the application
and remained in possession as of the hearingdate.Itwasalsouncontestedthatthemonthlyrentwas$1,185.13untilitincreasedto
$1,211.20effectiveOctober1,2020.
8. For the event that the
Unauthorized Occupants have already vacated by the time thisorderisreceived, Ihave calculatedthecompensation to August 20,2021:
(a)June6,2020 –September30,2020:117
daysx$38.27perday=$4,477.59
(b) October 1, 2020 –August 20, 2021: 324 days x 39.82 per day =
$12,901.68The total compensation
owingis $17,379.20.
9. After deducting the
rent deposit ($1,164.18) and interest owing on the rent deposit($6.46), the total amount the Unauthorized
Occupants owe the Landlord as of August 20,2021 is $16,208.63.
10.The Landlord collected a rent deposit of
$1,164.18 and this deposit is still being held bytheLandlord.
11.InterestisowingontherentdepositfromOctober1,2020toDecember31,2020.
12.The remaining issue is relief from
eviction. The Landlord was not aware of anycircumstances
of the Unauthorized Occupants that would favour relief from eviction andthe Unauthorized Occupants were not
present to provide evidence on this issue. In theabsence of such evidence, and where the Landlord advised me that
the UnauthorizedOccupants had agreed
to vacate the rental unit on August 20, 2021, my finding is that itwould be unfairtoprovide relief fromevictioninthe circumstances.
