13.It was uncontested that the Landlord
withheld consent to anassignmenton June 14,2020 on the basis that the applicant was employed full-time and was not
astudent. TheLandlord describes
herself as a “studenthousing provider”. She testified that she has nocontract forstudenthousing with any
educational institution nor does she have anydesignation or license to support this claim. She stated that it is her
preference to rent tostudentsonly.
The Landlord provided no basis for which she was entitled torefusethepotentialassigneeonJune14,2020.
14.I find therefusalto rent to an employed
young woman, rather than astudent, to beunreasonable
and arbitrary. The fact that the potential assignee was employed, and not astudent, is not a valid or reasonable
ground forrefusingher as an assignee. TheLandlord’s
preference to rent tostudents, does not permit her to discriminate against
non-studentapplicants, or even by
gender. Therefore, I find that the Landlord has unlawfullywithheld consent toassigntherentalunit contrary to theAct.
15.The Tenant seeks a termination of her
tenancy effective June 14, 2020. On the basis ofthe evidence in the hearing, I find that the Tenant did not
provide a formal notice oftermination
to the Landlord, pursuant to section 96 of the Act, entitling her to terminatethe tenancyonJune14,2020.
16.I have considered that the Tenant notified
the Landlord of her intention to terminate theLease Agreement by filing her A2 application with the Board, having
already moved herpossessions out of
the rental unit and returning her keys. I have also considered theundisputed fact that the Tenant’s
potential assignee advised that they were prepared totakeovertheleaseonJuly
1,2020.
17.Pursuant to subsection 98(3) of the Act, a
tenant or former tenant may apply to the Boardforanorderdeterminingthatthelandlordhasarbitrarilyorunreasonablywithheldconsent
to anassignment, and if the Board determines the landlord has unlawfully withheldconsent, theBoardmayorderthatthetenancybeterminated.
18.Having considered the evidence before the
Board, I find on a balance of probabilities thatthe rental unit could have beenassignedto the potential
assignee effective July 1, 2020,hadtheLandlordnotunreasonablywithheldconsent to theassignment.
19.On the facts before the Board, I find an
appropriate termination date for the tenancy isJuly1,2020.
