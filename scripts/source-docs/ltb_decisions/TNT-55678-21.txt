BACKGROUND

The Tenant applied under section 29(1)(b) alleging substantial interference with reasonable enjoyment arising from a dispute over the parking space assigned to the unit. The Landlord began assigning the parking spot to a different tenant starting August 2021, arguing that parking was not included in the lease, only in a verbal side agreement. The Tenant disputes this.

ANALYSIS

Section 22 of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 requires that any substantial interference with reasonable enjoyment be established on the evidence. The threshold for "substantial" interference is meaningful — not every inconvenience or dispute rises to the level requiring Board intervention.

The Tenant testified that parking was promised verbally but the lease is silent on this point. The Landlord maintains parking was never included. The Tenant has not produced any written confirmation of a parking agreement.

Where the evidence does not clearly establish that parking was part of the tenancy agreement, and the Tenant has alternative parking nearby, I cannot find that the loss of the parking spot constitutes a breach by the Landlord of section 22. The parking dispute is a civil matter arising from an alleged verbal agreement, not a Board matter under section 22.

I find the Tenant has not established substantial interference with reasonable enjoyment on the evidence before me.

The application is dismissed.

ORDER

1. The application is dismissed.
