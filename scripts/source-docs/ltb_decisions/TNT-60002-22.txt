ONTARIO LANDLORD AND TENANT BOARD
File Number: TNT-60002-22

BETWEEN:
Applicant Tenant
and
Respondent Landlord

REASONS FOR ORDER

BACKGROUND
The Tenant applied after the Landlord collected a "move-in deposit" of $500 described in the lease as a refundable amount held against damages, cleaning costs, or unpaid rent at the end of the tenancy, separate from the last month's rent deposit.

ANALYSIS
The tenancy agreement stated: "The Tenant shall pay a refundable move-in deposit of $500.00 upon execution of this agreement. This deposit will be held by the Landlord against any damages to the unit, cleaning costs, or rental arrears and returned within 30 days of the end of the tenancy, less any deductions."

The Board finds this clause void. Section 105(1) of the Act provides that a landlord shall not require a rent deposit greater than the lesser of one month's rent or the rent for one rental period, and a rent deposit must be applied to the last rental period.

A "move-in deposit" that functions as security against damages and cleaning costs is not a last month's rent deposit. The Act does not authorize any deposit for damages, cleaning, or arrears — these are matters to be resolved through the Board's application process if the parties cannot agree.

The Landlord argued that calling the deposit "refundable" made it distinguishable from the prohibited security deposits found in other cases. The Board rejects this. Whether a deposit is described as refundable does not change its legal character. It remains an amount held as security beyond what the Act permits.

PRINCIPLE
A move-in deposit, damage deposit, cleaning deposit, or any amount held as security against future tenant obligations beyond the permitted last month's rent deposit is void under section 105 of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006. Labelling a deposit "refundable" does not cure the statutory prohibition.

ORDER
The Landlord is ordered to repay the $500 move-in deposit in full, with interest at the guideline rate from the date it was collected.
