BACKGROUND

This application concerns an illegal rent increase imposed on a new tenancy. The previous tenant vacated in April 2022. The new Tenant signed a lease at $2,400/month commencing May 1, 2022. The previous tenant's lawful rent was $1,900/month. The Landlord argues that upon a new tenancy, rent control does not apply and the rent can be set at any amount.

ANALYSIS

Rent control under the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 applies to most residential units that were first occupied for residential purposes before November 15, 2018. This unit was first occupied in 2014. The rental unit is therefore subject to rent control, and the lawful rent is tied to the rent charged to the previous tenant plus any lawful guideline increases.

Section 136(1) provides that the maximum rent for a rental unit shall not exceed the lawful rent plus the prescribed guideline increase for that year. The Landlord cannot simply charge a new tenant a higher rent at the commencement of a new tenancy for a rent-controlled unit. The "vacancy decontrol" provisions applicable to post-November 15, 2018 units do not apply here.

The lawful maximum rent for this unit was $1,900 plus the applicable guideline increases for 2021 (0%) and 2022 (1.2%), yielding a lawful maximum of approximately $1,922.80. The Landlord charged $2,400 — a $477.20 monthly excess.

The Tenant is entitled to a rent rebate for the excess paid over the lawful maximum for the tenancy to date (May to September 2022 — 5 months): $477.20 × 5 = $2,386.00.

ORDER

1. The application is granted.
2. The lawful maximum rent for the unit is $1,922.80 per month.
3. The Landlord shall pay to the Tenant $2,386.00 as a rent rebate for excess rent paid from May through September 2022.
4. The Landlord shall pay to the Tenant $48.00 for the cost of filing the application.
5. The rent for this unit shall not exceed $1,922.80 per month until a lawful rent increase is effected pursuant to the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006.
