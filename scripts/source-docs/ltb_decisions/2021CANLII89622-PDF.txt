Homestead Land Holdings Limited (the 'Landlord') applied for an order to terminate the tenancy
of Ian Brown (the 'Tenant') and evict the Unauthorized Occupant.
This application was heard by videoconference on April 28, 2021.
Only the Landlord's Legal Representative C. Sankey attended the hearing.
Determinations:
1. The lease between the parties indicated that Ian Brown was the sole tenant in the rental
unit. (Exhibit 1) The Tenant died on June 18, 2020.
2. The Landlord testified that a handwritten note was received from the Tenant during the
course of the tenancy, requesting that Jody (Jodi) Andrews (‘JA’) be added as an
occupant to his lease.
3. The Landlord testified that after learning of the Tenant’s death they contacted JA to
determine whether she wished to enter into a lease agreement with the Landlord, JA
advised the Landlord that she did not wish to do so.
4. The Landlord testified that upon being notified of the Tenant’s death, and JA’s declining
to enter into a tenancy agreement, the Landlord filed an application with the Board to
terminate the tenancy between the Landlord and the Tenant and remove any
Unauthorized Occupants from the rental unit.
5. The JA was residing in the rental unit at the time the application was filed.
6. Since the Tenant’s death, the Landlord has not entered into a tenancy agreement for the
rental unit with any other parties.

7. Pursuant to subsection 91(1) of the Residential Tenancies’ Act, 2006, R.O. 2006, c.17
(‘the Act’), the tenancy is deemed terminated 30 days after the death of a tenant. By
virtue of subsection 91(1), I find that the tenancy between the Landlord and the Tenant
terminated 30 days after the Tenant died, on July 18, 2020.
8. It may be argued that as of June 19, 2020 JA could be forced to vacate the rental unit by
the Landlord and the Landlord could obtain vacant possession. However, in order to
protect the rights of all affected parties (the Landlord from possible liability and the
occupant from eviction without notice) I find it is more appropriate to apply section 100 to
the application. That provision allows a landlord to apply for an eviction order “if a tenant
transfers the occupancy of a rental unit to a person” in a manner that was not authorized
by the Act.
9. The automatic termination of a tenancy thirty days after the Tenant’s death pursuant to
section 91 of the Act, has been addressed in similar circumstances by the Courts. The
question as to whether a tenancy could be transferred to an unauthorized occupant by
death was addressed by the Divisional Court, who accepted such a scenario as a
possibility in Jemiola v. Firchuk, [2005] O.J. No. 6085.
10. On this basis, I find that section 100 of the Act applies as it speaks to the unauthorized
transfer of a tenancy and a Landlord’s right to evict a tenant and a person to whom
occupancy of the rental unit was transferred.
11. Therefore, while I find the tenancy terminated pursuant to section 91(1) of the Act, I also
find it appropriate to grant vacant possession under section 100 to address the
unauthorized occupancy by JA.
12. The Landlord did not seek compensation for occupation of the rental unit.
13. I have considered all of the disclosed circumstances in accordance with subsection
83(2) of the Act, and find that it would be unfair to grant relief from eviction pursuant
to subsection 83(1) of the Act.
It is ordered that:
1. The tenancy between the Landlord and the Tenant is terminated as of July 18, 2020.
2. The Unauthorized Occupant shall move out of the rental unit on or before May 15, 2021.
3. If the unit is not vacated by May 15, 2021 then starting May 16, 2021, the Landlord may
file this order with the Court Enforcement Office (the Sheriff), so that the eviction may be
enforced.
