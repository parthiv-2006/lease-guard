ONTARIO LANDLORD AND TENANT BOARD
File Number: TNT-10002-23

BETWEEN:
Applicant Tenant
and
Respondent Landlord

REASONS FOR ORDER

BACKGROUND
The Tenant applied under section 135 of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006, for an order requiring the Landlord to return money collected as a "re-letting fee" when the Tenant vacated before the end of a one-year fixed term.

ANALYSIS
The tenancy agreement provided: "Upon early termination by the Tenant, the Tenant shall pay all costs associated with re-letting the unit, including advertising fees and a re-letting administrative fee of $1,500."

The Board finds this clause void in its entirety under section 3 of the Act. The Act provides a closed list of permitted charges: rent, rent deposits, and key deposits within prescribed limits. A re-letting administrative fee is not among them.

Section 134(1) states that "no landlord shall, directly or indirectly, with respect to any rental unit, charge or collect any amount of money, including a fee, fine or penalty, other than rent." A re-letting fee falls squarely within this prohibition.

The Board notes the distinction between a re-letting fee (void) and actual proven losses for which a landlord may seek compensation by filing an application with the Board. The landlord is not without remedy — they may apply for compensation for unmitigated losses from the vacated unit. However, they may not collect a predetermined fee that operates as a financial penalty for early departure.

PRINCIPLE
Re-letting fees, administrative charges for early termination, and any predetermined financial penalties for a tenant vacating before the end of a fixed term are prohibited additional charges under section 134 of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006. The landlord may only seek actual, proven, mitigated losses through the Board application process.

ORDER
The Board orders the Landlord to repay the $1,500 re-letting fee collected from the Tenant, plus interest.
