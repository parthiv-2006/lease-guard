13.The relevantcaselawisclearthatthetestofgoodfaithisgenuineintentiontooccupytheresidential unit and not
the reasonableness of the landlord’s proposal:*Feeney v. Noble*(1994), 19, O.R. (3d) (Div. Ct.). As
confirmed in subsequent decisions (*Salter v. Beljinac*,2001 CanLII 30231(ONSC DC)), this legal
test remains unchanged and the “good faith”requirement
simply means that the Landlord sincerely intends to occupy the rental unit.While the good faith of the Landlord
remains the test to be applied, I may also drawinferences aboutthe
Landlord’s goodfaithfromtheLandlord’sconductandmotives(*Favav.Harrison*,[2014ONSC3352](https://www.canlii.org/en/on/onscdc/doc/2014/2014onsc3352/2014onsc3352.html)(ONSCDC).
14.The factthe
Landlorddidnotmoveintotherentalunitisnotthesoleconsiderationwhendetermining
his good faith intentions when serving the Tenants an N12 notice.I mustconsideralltheevidenceandallcircumstances.
15.As notedabove,by virtueofsubsection 57(5)oftheActtheLandlordispresumedto havegiven the N12 in bad faith because he entered into a new tenancy
agreement shortly afterthe Tenants
moved out. However, for the reasons that follow, I find that the Landlord hasrebuttedthepresumptionandestablishedthattheN12wasnotgiveninbad
faith.
