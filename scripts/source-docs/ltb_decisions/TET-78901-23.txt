BACKGROUND

The Tenant applied alleging failure to maintain. The Tenant reported visible mold growth in the bathroom and bedroom of the rental unit in September 2022. The Landlord's agent attended, acknowledged the mold, and promised remediation. As of the hearing date in April 2023, the mold had not been remediated. The Tenant submitted photographs and an air quality test report showing elevated mold spore counts.

Additionally, the tenancy agreement contained a clause stating: "The Tenant shall be responsible for all maintenance and upkeep of the rental unit, including but not limited to plumbing, mold prevention, and appliance repairs."

ANALYSIS

Section 20(1) of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 requires the Landlord to maintain the residential complex in a good state of repair and fit for habitation. The presence of mold, confirmed by the Landlord's own agent, is a condition that renders a unit unfit for habitation and violates health and maintenance standards under Ontario Regulation 516/06.

The lease clause purporting to make the Tenant responsible for all maintenance, including mold prevention, is void. Section 3(1) of the Act provides that the Act applies despite any agreement to the contrary. Section 20(1) cannot be contracted out of. The Landlord's obligation to maintain the unit is non-delegable and a lease clause shifting that obligation to the Tenant has no legal effect whatsoever.

The Landlord's failure to remediate known mold for over seven months is a serious and prolonged breach of section 20. The Tenant suffered health concerns (documented by a physician's letter) and reduced enjoyment of the unit throughout this period.

I find an abatement of 25% of rent for the seven-month period (September 2022 to April 2023) is appropriate. At $1,750/month, that is $437.50/month × 7 months = $3,062.50.

ORDER

1. The application is granted.
2. The lease clause making the Tenant responsible for mold prevention and maintenance is void under sections 3(1) and 20 of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 and is of no legal effect.
3. The Landlord shall immediately remediate the mold in the rental unit at [UNIT] and shall provide the Tenant with a written report from a certified mold remediation company within 60 days.
4. The Landlord shall pay to the Tenant $3,062.50 as an abatement of rent.
5. The Landlord shall pay to the Tenant $48.00 for the cost of filing the application.
