# Determinations:
****
**
1. TheLandlordallegesthatthepartiessignedanN11agreementtoterminatethetenancy
on August 1, 2021.
**
2. The Tenant alleges that she was misled to sign the N11
agreement to terminate the tenancy under false pretences.The Tenant is a vulnerable, elderly woman,
and she allegesthatformerLandlordmisrepresentedthenatureofthedocumentshewassigning.
PreliminaryIssue,theTenant’sRentalUnit:
**
3. Theresidentialcomplexisahousethathasbeendividedintothreeseparateunits.
**
4. ItisundisputedthattheTenanthaslivedintheresidentialcomplexsince1994.The
residential complex was sold on orabout
July 2021, and the Tenantsigned the
N11 agreement to terminate with the former owner and Landlord, J. Dabu (JD).
5. It is unnecessary forme to determine the circumstancesin which the Tenant signed the N11agreementtoterminate,becauseIfindthattheN11agreementtoterminatedidnot
properly identify the rental unit for the reasons that follow.
6. ItisundisputedthattheTenanthasalwayslivedintheupperrentalunit.
7. The Tenant’s witnesses had photos of at least two
separate entrances to the residential complex.Thereisanoutsidedoortoenterthemainfloorrentalunit,andaseparatedoor toentertheupperrentalunit.Eachdoorhasaseparatemailbox,one with“91A”andone with “91B”.The parties allege that there is a basement rental unit, but I
did not hear evidence about whether it is rented, or who lives in that unit.
8. There were differing accounts of
how, and under what circumstances the Tenant’s daughterand her husband, L. Cinco (LC) and H. Ergun (HE), came to
reside in at least a portion of the main floor unit.The former Landlord, J. Dabu (JD), does not dispute that there
are two separate units.JD said that
he has personal belongings in the main floor unit,andthathelivedthereafewyearsago.However,itwasundisputedthattheTenant
never lived on the main floor.
9. TheN11agreementtoterminate,submittedwiththeLandlord’sL3application,statedthat
the name of the Tenant was S. Cinco, and the address of the rental unit is 91
KimberleyAve.
10.TheTenant’srentalunitcannotpossiblybe91KimberleyAve,asitisundisputedthatshe has never
resided in all of 91 Kimberley Ave, and there are at least two, and possibly
three rental units at 91 Kimberley Ave.
11.Subsection43(1)(a)ofthe*ResidentialTenanciesAct,2006*(the‘Act’),providesthatany notice of termination**shall**identify
the rental unit for which the notice is given.
12.TheN11agreementtoterminatefailedtoproperlyidentitytherentalunitforwhichthe
notice was given.
13.Subsection77(8)oftheActprovidesthatanorderbasedonanagreementtoterminate
may be set aside, if (a)(i) the landlord and tenant did not enter into an
agreement to terminate the tenancy.There
can be no lawful agreement to terminate a tenancy ifthe agreement does not contain a properly identified rental
unit.
14.TheevictionorderHOL-11908-21,issuedonSeptember13,2021,thatisbasedonthe unlawful agreement to terminate will
be set aside.
# Itis
orderedthat:
****
1. OrderHOL-11908-21,issuedonSeptember13,2021,issetaside.
2. TheLandlord'sapplicationisdismissed.
