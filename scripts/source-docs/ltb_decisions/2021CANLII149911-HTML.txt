ReasonsandAnalysis:
19.The Tenant provided no evidence that
proves, on a balance of probabilities, that there was any coercion or pressure
to sign the N11 agreements to terminate.The
forms were alwayssignedfirstbytheLandlords,droppedoffwiththeTenant,and hewasgivenupto
a few days to read, consider, and sign the forms.He said that he did not see a need to consult a lawyer as the
forms were straightforward.The
Tenant had the option of seeking legal assistance, and he chose not to exercise
that option.Instead, he freely
signedtheN11agreementstoterminate.Consequently,IdonotfindthattheTenanthas proved that there was no true consent, which would render the N11
agreements to terminate void*ab initio*.
20.The Tenant suggested that the parties
concluded a number of N11 agreements to terminate.The Landlords kept agreeing to prolong the date of termination,
and the TenantsubmitsthatitwasreasonabletoassumetheLandlordswouldprolongthedateof
termination further.
21.TheundisputedevidencewasthattheLandlordskeptextendingtheterminationdateat
the request of the Tenant.The Tenant
had not yet found a suitableplace
to live, so he kept asking for more time to leave.This fact alone does not obligate the Landlords to keep
extending the termination date further.Prior
forbearance does not act as an estoppel on a validly concluded consent to
terminate.
