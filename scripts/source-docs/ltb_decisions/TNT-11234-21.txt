BACKGROUND

The Tenant applied alleging an illegal rent increase. The Landlord served a Form N1 on October 14, 2020, providing notice of a 0% rent increase (no increase) effective January 1, 2021. The 2021 guideline was 0% due to a government freeze. The Tenant applied because the N1 form was served, alleging the process was improper.

ANALYSIS

A landlord who wishes to increase rent must serve a Form N1 under section 116 of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006, providing at least 90 days' notice. The Landlord here provided 79 days' notice from October 14 to January 1 — less than the required 90 days. However, the Landlord did not actually increase the rent — the N1 indicated a 0% increase, meaning the rent remains unchanged.

The purpose of section 116's notice requirement is to give tenants sufficient time to plan for a rent increase. Where no increase is being imposed, the harm to the tenant from technical non-compliance with the notice period is nil. The Board has discretion under section 202(2) to refuse to grant an application where there is no real prejudice to the applicant.

In this case, since no rent increase was charged and the 2021 guideline was 0% in any event, I find no material prejudice to the Tenant. The application is dismissed.

ORDER

1. The application is dismissed.
2. The Board notes that future rent increase notices, even for the guideline amount, must be served at least 90 days before the effective date using the approved Form N1.
