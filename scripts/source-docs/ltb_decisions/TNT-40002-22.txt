ONTARIO LANDLORD AND TENANT BOARD
File Number: TNT-40002-22

BETWEEN:
Applicant Tenant
and
Respondent Landlord

REASONS FOR ORDER

BACKGROUND
The Tenant applied for repayment of a "pet deposit" of $500 and a monthly "pet fee" of $50 collected by the Landlord as a condition of allowing the Tenant to keep a dog.

ANALYSIS
The tenancy agreement contained: "The Tenant shall pay a refundable pet deposit of $500.00 and a monthly pet fee of $50.00 as a condition of permission to keep one (1) dog in the rental unit."

The Board makes two findings.

First, the pet deposit is void under section 105 of the Act. Section 105 permits only a last month's rent deposit up to one month's rent. A pet deposit is not a last month's rent deposit and is not authorized by the Act. Calling it "refundable" does not bring it within any permitted category.

Second, the monthly pet fee is a prohibited additional charge under section 134(1). It is not rent as defined by the Act — or if it is intended to be rent, it would constitute a rent increase that must comply with the rent increase provisions in Part VII of the Act. A landlord cannot impose a separate monthly fee for keeping a pet; the right to keep pets is protected by section 14 and cannot be made conditional on payment of a fee beyond lawful rent.

Taken together, these clauses attempt to monetize the section 14 right to keep pets, which is void.

PRINCIPLE
A pet deposit and a monthly pet fee are both unlawful: the deposit violates section 105 and the monthly fee violates section 134(1) of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006. A landlord cannot charge additional fees as a condition of permitting pets when section 14 already prohibits no-pet clauses.

ORDER
The Landlord is ordered to repay the $500 pet deposit and all monthly pet fees collected, with interest.
