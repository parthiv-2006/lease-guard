BACKGROUND

The Tenant applied under section 135 of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 for the return of money illegally collected. Prior to moving into the rental unit at [ADDRESS], Hamilton, Ontario, the Landlord required the Tenant to pay a $3,000 "security deposit" in addition to the first and last month's rent. The monthly rent was $1,450. The Landlord characterized the payment as a "damage deposit" to cover potential damage to the unit beyond normal wear and tear.

ANALYSIS

Section 105(1) of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 limits the deposit a landlord may collect to a rent deposit not exceeding one month's rent, to be applied to the last rental period. Section 134(1) prohibits a landlord from charging or collecting any amount from a tenant as a condition of granting or continuing a tenancy, other than the rent and a key deposit under s. 105 and 106. There is no provision in the RTA permitting a landlord to collect a "damage deposit," "security deposit," or any other deposit beyond the last month's rent.

The Landlord's collection of a $3,000 "security deposit" directly violates section 105(1) and section 134(1) of the RTA. The label the Landlord applied to the payment — "damage deposit" rather than "security deposit" — is of no legal significance. Any amount collected from a tenant beyond the one-month rent deposit permitted by the Act is an illegal charge.

The Landlord must repay the full $3,000 to the Tenant. The collection was illegal and there is no discretion to reduce this amount based on actual damage to the unit. The Landlord's remedy for damage beyond normal wear and tear is a claim under section 89 of the RTA, not an upfront deposit.

ORDER

1. The application is granted.
2. The Landlord shall pay to the Tenant the sum of $3,000.00, being the illegal security deposit collected contrary to sections 105(1) and 134(1) of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006.
3. The Landlord shall pay the Tenant $48.00 for the cost of filing the application.
4. The total amount the Landlord shall pay is $3,048.00.
