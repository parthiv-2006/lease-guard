# PreliminaryIssues:
****
1.The notice of
termination in this matter (N12) was served by an Andrew Ritchie, who wastheLandlordat thetimeof service.The terminationwasforpurchaser’sown use.

3.The determination date
in the notice of termination was May 31, 2020. Section 55.1 of the*Residential Tenancies Act, 2006*(the
‘Act’) states that “the landlord shallcompensatethetenant no later than on the termination date specified in the
notice of termination”.Compensationisanamountofmoney equaltoonemonth’srent.
4. Mr.RitchiedidnotcompensatetheTenantsbetweenserviceoftheN12andclosing.
5.After closing, the
Landlord, herein, Ms. McKenna, did notcompensatethe Tenants on orbefore the termination date in the notice
of termination. In fact, as of the hearing date,April9, 2021, the
Landlord stillhadnot paidcompensationtotheTenants.
6.Ms.Morris,onbehalfoftheLandlord,expressedapreparednessonthepartoftheLandlord
to pay onemonth’srentto
theTenantsforthwith.
7.The Landlord has
brought a second application against these Tenants for non-payment ofrent (an L1). Ms. Morris was also
prepared to amend that application to claim one lessmonthofarrears, in
ordertomeettherequirementsofsection 55.1ofthe
Act.
8.Ms. Morris referred me
to EAl-86813-19 and EAL-89026-20 as authority for theproposition that waiver ofarrearssatisfied the requirement in

# Determinations:
****
**
1. Virtually,alltheequitiesinthismatterappeartofavourtheLandlord.
**
2. However, neither Mr.
Richie, while he remained landlord, nor Ms. McKenna, once shebecame the Landlord, complied with the
mandatory requirements of section 55.1 of theAct.
**
3. Some landlords appear
to read into section 55.1, words that are not there. The sectiondoes not say that thecompensationshall
be paid by the Landlord by the termination date*unless*the tenant is inarrears, or unless the tenant
stops paying rent after service of theN12,
or unless the tenant has indicated that he or she will not leave. There are no
suchqualifyingwords.
**
4. If a tenant is inarrearsbefore service of theN12, or stops paying rent after theN12isserved, then I fully understand that a
landlord may find it galling that he or she still has tocompensatethe tenant. However, again looking to section 55.1,
the section does not saythat the
landlord must deliver cash or a cheque to a tenant. A landlord couldcompensatea tenant by forgiving rent for one month.
Tactically, it could be prudent for a landlord toforgive amonththatisalreadyinarrears.
**
5. While the determination
ofarrearsbetween the parties is to be determined on anupcoming L1 hearing, and therefore I am not making any finding
as toarrears, there was*prima
facie*evidence of the Tenants
being many months inarrears, which includedarrearsforApril 2020.
**
6. If the Landlord had
forgiven the rent for April 2020, with respect to which there may havebeen some discussion, then I would have
found that section 55.1 of the Act had beensatisfied,
but the forgiveness would have had to be express and have occurred on orbefore May 31, 2021. As of the hearing of
this matter, the application forarrearscontinuedtoincludearrearsforApril 2020.
7. A landlord cannot claim
that a tenant has beencompensatedunder section 55.1 becausethe tenant has not paid rent for a given
month, and contemporaneously maintain anapplication
claimingadebtofarrearsforthatsamemonth.
8. Section 55.1 of the Act
is not discussed in EAL-89026-19. What is there in relation tocompensationistooscant toconstituteany statement oflaw.
9. EAL-89026-20isdistinguishableinthatitstatesthatonemonth’srent*hadbeen*waived.
10.If I understood Ms. Morris, the above
waiver may have been accepted well after thetermination date in the notice, despite nothing to that effect appearing
in the order.However, even if that
is so, I am not bound by another decision of the Board andrespectfully disagree that such a sequence
of events is compliance with section 55. 1 ofthe Act.
11.TheLandlord’sapplicationmustfailfornon-compliancewithsection55.1oftheAct.
