BACKGROUND

The Tenant applied alleging the Landlord entered the rental unit unlawfully on September 5, 2021 to conduct an annual inspection. The Landlord delivered written notice on September 3, 2021 specifying the reason as "annual property inspection" and the time as September 5, 2021 between 10:00 a.m. and 12:00 p.m. The Landlord entered at 10:30 a.m.

ANALYSIS

Section 26(1) of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 requires written notice of at least 24 hours before entry, stating the reason and time of entry. Section 27(1)(f) authorizes entry for inspection purposes.

The Landlord delivered written notice on September 3 for entry on September 5 — more than 24 hours in advance. The notice specified the reason (annual inspection) and a time window. Entry occurred within the specified window.

The Tenant argues that a two-hour window is too imprecise. However, the Act does not require entry at an exact time, only that the time be specified. A two-hour window provides the Tenant with sufficient information to plan accordingly and is consistent with the practical requirements of scheduling inspections.

I find that the Landlord complied with all requirements of section 26(1) of the RTA. The entry on September 5, 2021 was lawful.

The application is dismissed.

ORDER

1. The application is dismissed.
