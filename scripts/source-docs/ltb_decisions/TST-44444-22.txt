BACKGROUND

The Tenant applied alleging unlawful entry. The Landlord entered the unit on July 30, 2022 without any prior notice after the Tenant reported a water leak coming through the ceiling. The entry occurred at 9:15 a.m. and lasted approximately 20 minutes. The Landlord shut off the water supply and patched a burst pipe in the upstairs unit.

ANALYSIS

Section 26(1) of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 requires 24 hours' written notice before entry. However, section 26(3) provides a significant exception: a landlord may enter without notice in the case of an emergency.

The question is whether the circumstances here constituted an emergency for the purposes of section 26(3). The Tenant herself reported water actively leaking through the ceiling. A burst pipe causing active water damage to the unit is precisely the type of emergency contemplated by s. 26(3). Immediate access to shut off the water supply and prevent further damage to the unit, the Tenant's belongings, and the building structure was objectively necessary.

I find that the Landlord's entry on July 30, 2022 was authorized under the emergency exception in section 26(3) of the RTA. The entry was prompt, limited in scope to addressing the emergency, and was followed by proper repairs. This was not a situation of the Landlord using a pretext of emergency to circumvent the notice requirement.

The application is dismissed.

ORDER

1. The application is dismissed.
