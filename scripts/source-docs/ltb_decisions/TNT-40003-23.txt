ONTARIO LANDLORD AND TENANT BOARD
File Number: TNT-40003-23

BETWEEN:
Applicant Tenant
and
Respondent Landlord

REASONS FOR ORDER

BACKGROUND
The Tenant applied after the Landlord levied a fine of $200 per incident each time the Tenant's pet was observed in a common area of the residential complex, relying on a building rule incorporated by reference into the tenancy agreement.

ANALYSIS
The tenancy agreement incorporated building rules that stated: "Pets are not permitted in any common area of the building, including lobbies, elevators, hallways, and amenity spaces. Violations will result in a fine of $200.00 per incident, charged to the Tenant's account."

Whether the prohibition on pets in common areas is enforceable is a separate question from whether a fine may be levied for a violation. The Board addresses the fine.

Section 134(1) of the Act prohibits a landlord from charging or collecting any amount other than rent, including any fee, fine, or penalty. A $200 pet-in-common-area fine is precisely a fine or penalty prohibited by section 134. Building rules incorporated into a tenancy agreement do not stand in a different legal position from any other term of the agreement — they remain subject to section 3 of the Act.

The Landlord argued the fine was a reasonable deterrent and consistent with rules in other residential buildings. The reasonableness or prevalence of such rules is not a defence. Section 134 is categorical: no fines, no penalties.

PRINCIPLE
A fine for a pet appearing in a common area — or any fine, penalty, or monetary charge for a breach of a building rule — is a prohibited additional charge under section 134(1) of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006. Building rules incorporated into a tenancy agreement are subject to the same statutory constraints as any other lease term.

ORDER
The Landlord is ordered to repay all fines collected from the Tenant under the pet-in-common-area rule, plus interest.
