# Determinations:
****
****
**
1. The
Tenant was in possession of the rental unit on the date the application to
collect rentthe Tenant owes (L9)
was filed. The Landlord did*not*serve a Notice to Terminate theTenancy.
**
2. Thelawfulmonthlyrentis$1,353.47.
**
3. At the hearing it was
agreed by the parties that the Tenant gave vacant possession of therentalunittotheLandlord onOctober1,2020.
**
4. TheLandlordcollectedandisholdinga lastmonthrentdepositintheamountof
$1,200.00.
**
5. Theonlyissuetobedeterminedistherentarrearsowing,ifany.
**
6. TheLandlord states, itisnotdisputedthattheTenantgavenoticeintheformofanN9inlate
August 2020, to vacate the rental unit on September 30, 2020. The Landlord
statesthe Tenant gave notice of his
intention to end his tenancy, however, it was not therequired60daysnoticeasperthe*ResidentialTenanciesAct,2006*andwastherefore
not accepted*.*The Landlord is seeking rent for the period of October 1 to October 31,2020,which wouldsatisfy therequired60 days notice.
**
7. The Landlord states
that due to the corporate Landlord’s head office being relocatedsome of their documentation has been
misplaced. The letter demonstrating that the earlytermination of the tenancy was not accepted by the Landlord is
not available, however, itis the
Landlord’s standard practice to not accept short notice of termination and to
send astandard letter to the
Tenant. Further, the Landlord relies on the standard Board N9 formwhichspecificallyindicatesthat60daysnoticeisrequired
toterminate atenancy.
**
8. The Tenant disputes the
rent arrears because the Landlord, through the buildingsuperintendent, accepted his notice to end the tenancy and the
last month rent depositshouldbeappliedto September2020,andheshouldnotoweanyrentforOctober2020.
**
9. The Tenant submits that
in late August 2020 he provided notice to the buildingsuperintendentof hisintentiontovacatetherentalunitonSeptember30, 2020,whereasa handicapped unit had become available.
Initially the notice was verbal and he believedthe superintendent accepted his notice. The Tenant states there
were renovationsongoing in the
residential complex and believed the early vacancy would grant theLandlord time to renovate the unit prior
to it being occupied by a new tenant. When hefollowed upwith thesuperintendentafewdayslater
thesuperintendent gave him anN9to
complete and return. The Tenant states he did return the N9 on September 1stin lieu ofhis rent, believing his last month rent deposit would cover most of the
rent and told thesuperintendent to
let him know the balance owing. He heard nothing further from theLandlord untilreceivinga Noticeof Hearing fromthe Board.
**
10.The Landlord states that the building
superintendent does not have the authority toapprove a Tenant’s termination of tenancy, and that all approval’s come
from the HeadofficeandthatallTenants
areawareof this policy.
**
11.The Tenant disputes that the superintendent
did not have the authority to accept the earlytermination of his tenancy, whereas it was through a verbal agreement
with thesuperintendent that he was
able to rent a storage locker, with the subsequent $20.00increase inrentappearing eachmonththereafter.
**
12.The Landlord was unable to provide a copy
of the Tenant’s lease agreement or anydocumentation
that would demonstrate the Tenant has at any time been informed of thelimitations of the superintendent’s authority.
However, the Tenant was also unable toprovide
any supporting evidence, including testimony of the superintendent, todemonstratethat his notice toendhistenancyearlyhad infactbeen accepted.
**
13.I note that although the superintendent did
hear the Tenant’s intention to end the tenancyearly, and did take physical possession of the Tenant’s N9, this is not
indicative ofapprovaloftheTenant’s request.
**
14.I find that in the absence of supporting
evidence to demonstrate whether the Tenant’snotice to terminate his tenancy early was or was not accepted by the
Landlord, I must relyonsubsection44(2)ofthe*ResidentialTenanciesAct,2006*(the‘Act’),whichstatesthat
a notice to
terminate a monthly tenancy shall be given at least 60 days before the datethe termination is specified to be
effective, and at the end of a rental period. I find that theTenant did not provide adequate notice to
terminate the tenancy in accordance with theAct, and therefore the Tenant owes the lawful monthly rent for September
and October2020.
**
15.I find that the Tenant owes to the Landlord
$1,707.94. This amount includes $1,353.47 foreach September and October 2020,**minus the last month's rent deposit
of $1,200.00**,plusthe cost offilingthis
applicationintheamountof$201.00.

4. TheTenantsshallalsopaytotheLandlord$201.00forthecostoffilingtheapplication.
5. If the Tenants do not
