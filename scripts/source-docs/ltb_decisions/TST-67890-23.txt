BACKGROUND

The Tenant applied under section 29(1)(b) of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 alleging repeated unlawful entry by the Landlord into the rental unit. The Tenant seeks an abatement of rent and an order prohibiting future unlawful entries.

The rental unit is a basement apartment at [ADDRESS], Mississauga, Ontario. The monthly rent is $1,950. The tenancy commenced January 2021.

The Tenant presented a log documenting eight unauthorized entries between October 2022 and March 2023. The Tenant installed a security camera at the entry door and produced video footage confirming five of those entries. On all occasions, the entries occurred without any written notice delivered in advance. The Landlord would enter and either inspect the furnace room or check the smoke detectors.

The Landlord testified that the lease agreement signed by the Tenant stated the Landlord could enter "at any time for inspection purposes." The Landlord argued this contractual provision authorized the entries.

ANALYSIS

The provision in the lease permitting entry "at any time for inspection purposes" is void and unenforceable. Section 3(1) of the RTA provides that the Act applies despite any agreement or waiver to the contrary. The parties to a tenancy agreement cannot contract out of the notice requirements in section 26 of the Act. A lease term purporting to authorize entry without notice has no legal effect.

Section 26(1) requires at least 24 hours' written notice before entry, specifying the reason and time of entry. Routine inspection is a permitted reason for entry under s. 27(1)(f), but it still requires proper written notice. The Landlord's entry for "inspection" without notice therefore violated s. 26(1) on each occasion.

I accept the Tenant's evidence of five video-confirmed entries and three additional entries from the log, totalling eight unlawful entries. The repeated nature of the violation and the Landlord's reliance on a void lease provision as justification is an aggravating factor.

The Tenant suffered a loss of privacy and quiet enjoyment over a prolonged period. I find an abatement of $200 per unlawful entry to be appropriate, totalling $1,600.

ORDER

1. The application is granted.
2. The clause in the tenancy agreement permitting the Landlord to enter "at any time" is void and unenforceable under section 3(1) of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006.
3. The Landlord shall pay the Tenant $1,600.00 as an abatement of rent.
4. The Landlord shall pay the Tenant $48.00 for the cost of filing the application.
5. The Landlord shall cease entering the rental unit except in compliance with sections 26 and 27 of the RTA.
